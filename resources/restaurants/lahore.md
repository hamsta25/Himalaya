## Must-Try Lahore Food Street Experiences

### 1. Gawalmandi Food Street ⭐ ICONIC
- **Location:** Gawalmandi, Old Lahore
- **Timing:** Evening (6 PM onwards, best after 8 PM)
- **Price Range:** $3-10 per person
- **Specialty:** Traditional Lahori street food in historic setting
- **Must Try:**
  - Taka Tak (spicy brain/kidney curry)
  - Paya (goat trotters curry)
  - Nihari (slow-cooked meat stew)
  - Haleem (meat and lentil stew)
  - Fresh Naan from tandoor
- **Atmosphere:** Bustling, authentic, historic lanes
- **How to Reach:** Uber/Careem from hotel (₨300-500)
- **Pro Tip:** Go hungry, eat at multiple stalls, very spicy!

### 2. Fort Road Food Street
- **Location:** Opposite Badshahi Mosque
- **Timing:** Evening till late night
- **Price Range:** $5-15 per person
- **Specialty:** More upscale food street experience
- **Must Try:**
  - BBQ and grilled meats
  - Karahi (wok-cooked meat)
  - Various rice dishes
- **Atmosphere:** Open-air restaurants with Badshahi Mosque view
- **Better for:** Those wanting food street vibes with more comfort

## Traditional Lahori Restaurants

### 1. Butt Karahi ⭐ LEGENDARY
- **Location:** Lakshmi Chowk (original) - Multiple branches
- **Timing:** Lunch and Dinner
- **Price:** $8-15 per person
- **Specialty:** Mutton Karahi (their signature dish)
- **Must Order:**
  - Mutton Karahi (by weight - order 1kg for 2-3 people)
  - Fresh Naan
  - Lassi (yogurt drink)
- **Atmosphere:** Basic, focus on food
- **Reservation:** Not needed, expect wait at dinner time
- **Pro Tip:** Original Lakshmi Chowk location is best

### 2. Café Aylanto ⭐ UPSCALE
- **Location:** MM Alam Road
- **Timing:** Lunch and Dinner
- **Price:** $15-25 per person
- **Specialty:** Mediterranean and Continental
- **Why Visit:** Break from heavy Pakistani food, elegant ambiance
- **Good for:** Nice dinner, date night
- **Reservation:** Recommended, especially dinner

### 3. Haveli Restaurant
- **Location:** Near Badshahi Mosque, rooftop in old haveli
- **Timing:** All day
- **Price:** $10-20 per person
- **Specialty:** Traditional Pakistani in heritage setting
- **Must Try:** Lamb chops, local curries
- **Highlight:** AMAZING view of Badshahi Mosque from rooftop
- **Reservation:** Recommended for rooftop seating
- **Pro Tip:** Go for sunset/evening for best views and lighting

## Breakfast & Brunch Spots

### 1. Anarkali Food Street (Morning)
- **Location:** Anarkali Bazaar
- **Timing:** 6 AM - 12 PM
- **Price:** $2-5
- **Specialty:** Traditional Lahori breakfast
- **Must Try:**
  - Halwa Puri (sweet semolina with fried bread)
  - Nihari and Naan
  - Lassi
  - Channay (chickpea curry)
- **Atmosphere:** Very local, authentic experience

### 2. Bundu Khan
- **Location:** Multiple locations across Lahore
- **Timing:** All day
- **Price:** $8-15 per person
- **Specialty:** BBQ, Karahi, traditional Pakistani
- **Why Visit:** Reliable, consistent quality, clean
- **Good for:** Comfortable Pakistani food experience

## Sweet Shops & Desserts

### 1. Fazal Sweets
- **Location:** Main Boulevard, Gulberg
- **Specialty:** Traditional Pakistani sweets (mithai)
- **Must Try:**
  - Gulab Jamun
  - Ras Malai
  - Barfi
  - Traditional rice pudding (kheer)
- **Price:** $3-8 per kg
- **Good for:** Dessert, gifts to bring home

### 2. Yousaf Bhai Kulfi (Ice Cream)
- **Location:** Anarkali
- **Specialty:** Traditional kulfi (dense ice cream)
- **Flavors:** Pistachio, Mango, Almond
- **Price:** $1-2 per serving
- **Pro Tip:** Try Falooda (kulfi with vermicelli)

## Local Experiences

### 1. Lakshmi Chowk Area
- **What:** Historic food area with multiple small eateries
- **Try:** Phajja Siri Paya (famous for Siri Paya - head and trotters)
- **Timing:** Early morning or dinner
- **Atmosphere:** Very local, authentic Lahori experience
- **Price:** $3-7 per person

### 2. MM Alam Road
- **What:** Modern dining street
- **Options:** Dozens of cafes, restaurants, fast food
- **Good for:** Variety, modern ambiance, people watching
- **Price Range:** $8-25 per person

## Food Safety Tips

1. **Stick to Busy Places:** High turnover = fresh food
2. **Drink Bottled Water:** Always
3. **Avoid Raw Vegetables:** At street stalls
4. **Spice Level:** Lahori food is VERY spicy - ask for "medium" or "mild"
5. **Sanitize:** Carry hand sanitizer
6. **Ice:** Avoid ice in drinks at street food
7. **Trust Your Gut:** If place looks questionable, skip it

## Final Tips

1. **Go Hungry:** Lahori portions are HUGE
2. **Share Dishes:** Order 1-2 dishes for 2-3 people
3. **Naan is Free/Cheap:** Fill up on excellent bread
4. **Timing:** Street food best after 8 PM
5. **Dress Modestly:** Especially in old city areas
6. **Bargaining:** Not for food, but for street shopping nearby
7. **Cash:** Many places don't accept cards, keep PKR handy
