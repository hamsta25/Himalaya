## Currency & Exchange

### Pakistan (Pakistani Rupee - PKR)
- **Exchange Rate:** ~280-300 PKR = 1 USD (check current rate)
- **Where to Exchange:**
  - Airport (convenient but lower rates)
  - Banks (better rates, need time)
  - Authorized money exchangers (good rates)
- **ATMs:** Widely available in cities, carry backup cash
- **Credit Cards:** Accepted in hotels, malls, upscale restaurants

### Uzbekistan (Uzbek Som - UZS)
- **Exchange Rate:** ~12,000-12,500 UZS = 1 USD (check current rate)
- **Where to Exchange:**
  - Airport or banks in Tashkent
  - Exchange USD for best rates
- **Credit Cards:** Accepted in major establishments
- **Cash:** More useful in markets and smaller venues

**Recommended Cash Breakdown:**
- Carry: $500-1,000 USD cash (small denominations)
- Exchange to local currency as needed
- Keep emergency USD ($200-300) for contingencies
- Use credit cards where accepted for safety

## Money-Saving Tips

### Before You Go
1. **Book Early:** Flights and hotels cheaper 8-12 weeks in advance
2. **Flexible Dates:** Mid-week flights often cheaper
3. **Package Deals:** Tour operators may offer better rates for multi-day packages
4. **Travel Insurance:** Compare multiple providers
5. **Credit Card:** Use card with no foreign transaction fees

### During the Trip
1. **Accommodation:**
   - Consider guesthouses in smaller cities
   - Book direct with hotel (sometimes cheaper than booking sites)
   - Negotiate for extended stays

2. **Food:**
   - Eat where locals eat (better food, lower prices)
   - Hotel breakfast included saves money
   - Mix street food with restaurant meals
   - Buy bottled water in bulk from shops vs. tourist spots

3. **Transportation:**
   - Share costs if traveling with others
   - Use Uber/Careem in cities (often cheaper than taxis)
   - Negotiate daily driver rate vs. distance-based

4. **Activities:**
   - Free attractions are often the best (mosques, viewpoints)
   - Share guide costs with other travelers
   - Skip "tourist trap" activities

5. **Shopping:**
   - Bargain in markets (can save 30-50%)
   - Compare prices before buying
   - Buy local items in local areas (cheaper than tourist zones)

## Payment Methods Summary

| Method | Pros | Cons | Best Use |
|--------|------|------|----------|
| **Credit Card** | Safe, rewards, emergency credit | Fees, not always accepted | Hotels, flights, major purchases |
| **Debit Card** | ATM access, lower fees | Daily limits, theft risk | Cash withdrawals |
| **USD Cash** | Universally accepted, backup | Theft/loss risk | Exchange, emergencies |
| **Local Currency Cash** | Needed for many transactions | Can't use outside country | Daily expenses, markets |
| **Mobile Payment** | Convenient | Limited acceptance | Where available |

**Recommended Mix:**
- 30% cash (mix of USD and local currency)
- 70% credit/debit cards
- Keep payment methods in separate locations (wallet, hotel safe, hidden)
